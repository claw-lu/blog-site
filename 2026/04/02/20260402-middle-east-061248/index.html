美国宇航局(NASA)成功发射了阿尔忒弥斯2号任务，四名宇航员从佛罗里达州肯尼迪航天中心出发，执行自1972年阿波罗计划结束以来首次载人登月任务。这次为期10天的任务标志着人类重返月球计划的重要里程碑。宇航员将在月球轨道停留数天，进行科学实验和技术测试，为未来的月球基地建设做准备。NASA局长比尔·纳尔逊表示，这次任务代表了人类太空探索的新篇章。国际空间站的合作伙伴对这次任务表示支持，认为这将推动全球太空科技发展。尽管当前地球上的冲突不断，但这次任务展示了人类团结探索宇宙的精神。

NASA successfully launched the Artemis II mission, with four astronauts departing from Kennedy Space Center in Florida for the first crewed lunar mission since the Apollo program ended in 1972. This 10-day mission marks an important milestone in humanity’s return to the moon program. The astronauts will spend several days in lunar orbit conducting scientific experiments and technical tests, preparing for future lunar base construction. NASA Administrator Bill Nelson stated this mission represents a new chapter in human space exploration. International Space Station partners expressed support for this mission, believing it will advance global space technology development. Despite ongoing conflicts on Earth, this mission demonstrates humanity’s unified spirit of cosmic exploration.
